// FOUNDATIONS

What is backtesting?

Simulating a trading strategy against historical market data to evaluate how it would have performed — without risking a cent.

01 — concept

Replay the market

Your strategy's rules are applied to historical price data tick-by-tick. Every signal, entry, exit, and position size is evaluated as it would have been executed in real trading.

02 — purpose

Validate before you alocate

A strategy that looks brilliant in theory can collapse against real data. Backtesting exposes over-fitting, survivorship bias, and regime fragility before real capital is committed.

03 — limitations

Past ≠ future, but still matters

Slippage, market impact, and structural regime changes mean past performance is conditional — not predictive. QuantFrame models realistic friction including bid-ask spreads and commission.

04 — iteration

Strategy is a loop, not a line

Hypothesize, backtest, analyze, refine. Every run surfaces something to improve. Over dozens of iterations, edge sharpens and risk becomes legible.
